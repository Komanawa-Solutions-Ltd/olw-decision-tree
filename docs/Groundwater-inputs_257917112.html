OLW Monitoring Design Framework : Groundwater inputs

Created by Simon Linke, last modified on Jan 18, 2021

Groundwater inputs can influence nutrient budgets, especially N budgets. A key consideration for groundwater inputs is that they can ‘extend catchments’ - ie the true catchment is greater than just a delineated surface water catchment and hence inputs can be underestimated.

In the case of dune lakes that have limited in- and outflow, nutrient budgets can be dominated by groundwater budgets. If measured, total tributary inputs are less than expected based on catchment
rainfall and evapotranspiration; this indicates that additional inputs are likely to enter the lake via groundwater.

The concentrations associated with the groundwater can be measured using bores, piezometers, and seepage meters, or they can be estimated from catchment models - given the aquifers are adequately mapped and exchange is properly quantified. While typically groundwater can have elevated levels of nitrogen (as nitrate or ammonium), under some circumstances (old or deoxygenated groundwater) it
can also carry significant quantities of phosphorus.
