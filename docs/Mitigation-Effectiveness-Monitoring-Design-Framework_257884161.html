OLW Monitoring Design Framework : Mitigation Effectiveness Monitoring Design Framework

Created by Olivier Ausseil, last modified on Mar 14, 2021

  1. Step 1: Assembling catchment and mitigation information

When designing a monitoring plan to evaluate the effectiveness of a mitigation plan, the first step is to assemble data, information and knowledge about the mitigation programme being considered, and the area and catchment to which it is applied, including:

  • Information relative to the biophysical characteristics of the area of interest and associated catchment: location, climate, water and contaminant pathways and processes, biological communities, etc.

  • The Freshwater Values associated with the catchment

  • Information relative to the current state and pressures of the freshwater resource and associated catchment including past land use and land management changes

  • Details of the Mitigation Plan, including its objectives, measures of success, mitigation action type(s) and location

The above information is user-defined and considered an input into the Monitoring Design DST.

Step 2: Identify the Freshwater Attributes and potential effectiveness of mitigation plan

Once this information is assembled, the following steps are taken:

  • Define the freshwater Attributes and Indicators of relevance by combining those that are of significance to:

    • the freshwater values and environment type

    • the mitigation plan’s objectives and the mitigation actions being implemented

  • Estimate the potential effectiveness of the mitigation action at various location, by considering:

    • the mitigation action type(s) and location(s)

    • the mitigation action’s establishment time

    • the potential effects of each mitigation action at the local scale (e.g. % reduction of contaminant loads at source)

    • the potential effects of all the mitigation actions located upstream/upgradient

    • lag times, including transport times between source and receptor and biological response times/ hysteresis

Two information repositories have been assembled to aid in this process:

  • Freshwater Values and Attributes Table

  • Mitigations Actions Table

Step 3: Select Relevant Guidance/DST

For In-lake mitigations, go to the lake DST

For edge of field mitigations, go to specific monitoring guidance

For broader catchment mitigation plans (e.g. riparian management, erosion control, Farm Environment Plan processes), go first to the Guidance on spatial and temporal information then to the Riparian DST or Nitrate/groundwater DST
